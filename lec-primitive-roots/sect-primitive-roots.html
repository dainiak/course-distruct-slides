A primitive root in \(\bbZ_m\) is a residue \(r\) such that \(\{r^0,\,r^1,\,\dots,\,r^{p-2}\}=\bbZ_m^*\).

In other words, \(\ord r = \euler(m)\).

Existence of primitive roots modulo prime
For \(p\in\bbP\), there are exactly \(\euler(p-1)\) primitive roots in \(\bbZ_p\).
Existence of primitive roots modulo composite numbers
For \(m\notin\bbP\cup\{4\}\), primitive roots exist in \(\bbZ_m\) if and only if \(m = p^\ell\) or \(m=2p^\ell\) for \(p\in\bbP\setminus\{2\}\) and \(\ell\in\bbN\).
Note! A primitive root in \(\bbZ_{p^\ell}\) is not the same thing as a primitive element in \(\bbF_{p^\ell}\), since the structure of this field is different from the structure of the residue ring.
Let \(\gamma\) be a primitive root modulo \(p\). Then

\(\exists t: \quad (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} = 1 + pu\), where \(p\notdivides u\).

The residue \(\gamma + pt\) will be a primitive root modulo \(p^\ell\) for any \(\ell\).

Let \(q:=(\gamma^{p-1} - 1)/p \in\bbZ\) (FLT) , and let \(t=1+\gamma q\).

We have \[ \begin{array}{rl} (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} &\step{= \gamma^{p-1} + (p-1)\cdot \gamma^{p-2}\cdot pt + p^2\cdot \any} \\ &\step{= 1 + p\cdot (q-\gamma^{p-2}\cdot t + p\cdot \any)}\\ &\step{= 1 + p\cdot (q-\gamma^{p-2} - \gamma^{p-1}\cdot q + p\cdot \any)}\\ &\next{= 1 + p\cdot (-\gamma^{p-2} + p\cdot \any)} \end{array} \]

\[ (\gamma+pt)^{p-1} = 1 + pu,\text{ where } p\notdivides u. \] Then \[ \begin{array}{rl} (\gamma+pt)^{p\cdot(p-1)} & = (1 + pu)^p = 1 + p^2u + p^4\cdot\any\\ & \next{= 1 + p^2 u_1, \text{ where } p\notdivides u_1. } \end{array} \]

Similarly, for any \(k\in\bbN\) we have \[ (\gamma+pt)^{p^k\cdot(p-1)} = 1 + p^{k+1} u_k, \text{ where } p\notdivides u_k. \]

Let \(\beta := \gamma+pt\). For any \(k\in\bbN\) we have \[ \beta^{p^k\cdot(p-1)} = 1 + p^{k+1} u_k\;\neqmod{p^{k+2}}\; 1. \]

Let \(\ord\beta=s\) in \(\bbZ_{p^\ell}\). Then \[ s\;\divides\; \card{\bbZ_{p^\ell}^*} = \euler(p^\ell)=p^{\ell-1}(p-1). \]

On the other hand, \[ \beta^s \eqmod{p^\ell} 1 \implies \beta^s \eqmod{p} 1 \next{\implies (p-1)\divides s.}\]

Therefore, \(s=p^k(p-1)\), where \(0\le k\le\ell-1\).

But if \(k\le \ell-2\), then \(\beta^s\neqmod{p^{k+2}} 1\implies \beta^s\neqmod{p^\ell} 1\).

Hence, \(\beta\) is a primitive root modulo \(p^\ell\).

Let \(\beta\) be a primitive root modulo \(p^\ell\), and let \(\alpha\) be the odd one among \(\beta,\,\beta+p^\ell\).

We have \[ \euler(2p^\ell)\step{=2p^\ell\bigl(1-\tfrac{1}{2}\bigr)\bigl(1-\tfrac{1}{p}\bigr)}\step{=p^{\ell-1}(p-1)}\step{=\euler(p^\ell).} \]

For any \(k\) we have \[\alpha^k\eqmod{2p^\ell} 1 \iff \alpha^k\eqmod{p^\ell} 1\]

Therefore, \(\ord \alpha = \euler(2p^\ell)\) in \(\bbZ_{2p^\ell}\).
