Lagrange's Theorem (1768).
If \(\bbF\) is a field, \(P\in \bbF[x]\) and \(\deg P = d\), then \(P\) has at most \(d\) roots.
Proof: induction on \(d\).

If \(a\) is a root of polynomial \(P\in \bbF[x]\), and \(\deg P = d\), then \(P\) is divisible by \((x-a)\), that is \(P=(x-a)\cdot Q\), where \(\deg Q = d-1\).

By the inductive hypothesis, \(Q\) has at most \(d-1\) roots, therefore \(P\) has at most \(d\) roots.
